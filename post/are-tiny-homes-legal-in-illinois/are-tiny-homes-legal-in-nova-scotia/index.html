  • Zoning Classifications: Local municipalities have the authority to designate zoning classifications that dictate permitted land uses. These classifications can range from residential to commercial, industrial, and agricultural zones.   • Size and Height Requirements: Zoning regulations often impose limitations on the size and height of structures, including tiny homes. Ensuring your tiny home has a sturdy and compliant foundation is crucial for long-term stability and durability.

  • Water and Sewer Connections: Access to reliable water and sewer services is paramount for tiny homes. Local regulations may stipulate the type of water and sewer systems permitted, whether they be municipal connections, private wells, or septic tanks.
